####  **3.2.1. The Topoknot Candidate and the Failure of Thermal Production**

2.  As the universe expands and cools, their annihilation rate drops.

The final relic abundance is inversely proportional to the particle\'s annihilation cross-section:

Ω_DM h² ∝ 1 / \<σv\>

Where \<σv\> is the thermally-averaged annihilation cross-section. To match the observed dark matter abundance of Ω_DM h² ≈ 0.12, a thermal relic requires a cross-section of a specific size, often called the \"weak-scale cross-section,\" which is approximately 2.2 × 10⁻²⁶ cm³/s.

Ω_TK / Ω_DM = \<σv\>\_req / \<σv\>\_pred \>\> 1

If the Topoknot were a thermal relic, it would have been produced in such vast quantities that the universe\'s total density would be many times greater than what is observed---a catastrophic overclosure.

**3.3.1. The Dark Glueball Candidate and the Need for Self-Interaction**

If dark matter particles can scatter off one another, they can exchange energy and \"thermalize\" the central region of the halo. This process naturally flattens the cusp into a core, resolving the tension with observations. The required interaction strength is approximately:\
σ/m ≈ 0.1 - 1.0 cm²/g

The Topoknot, with its extremely small interaction cross-section, cannot provide this effect. This motivates the existence of a second, lighter, and self-interacting dark matter component.

- **Key Property:** As a composite particle of a strong-like force, it naturally possesses a large self-interaction cross-section, making it an ideal candidate to solve the small-scale structure problems.

Therefore, the second component of our dark sector is not an ad-hoc addition, but a natural consequence of the rich symmetry-breaking patterns of the fundamental Ω-field. Its purpose is to provide the necessary self-interaction that the primary Topoknot component lacks.
