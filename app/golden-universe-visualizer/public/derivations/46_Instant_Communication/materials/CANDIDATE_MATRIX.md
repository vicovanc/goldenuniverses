# Materials Candidate Matrix

## Candidate Classes

### A) Pi-Stacked Molecular Arrays

- expected channel strength: medium-high
- decoherence risk: medium
- manufacturability: medium
- instrumentation complexity: medium
- rationale: direct alignment with pi-stacking phase-memory channel assumptions

### B) Engineered Phase-Phonon Crystal Systems

- expected channel strength: medium
- decoherence risk: low-medium
- manufacturability: medium-low
- instrumentation complexity: high
- rationale: strong control over phase-phonon propagation and spectral shaping

### C) Hybrid Bioelectric + d-Orbital Interfaces

- expected channel strength: high (hypothesized)
- decoherence risk: high
- manufacturability: low
- instrumentation complexity: very high
- rationale: potentially strongest coupled channel but highest integration risk

## Scoring Rubric

Each class is scored (1-5) on:

- channel gain potential
- coherence persistence
- fabrication feasibility
- repeatability
- safety and operational stability

Weighted score:

`score = 0.30*gain + 0.25*coherence + 0.20*fabrication + 0.15*repeatability + 0.10*safety`

## Initial Prioritization

1. Pi-stacked molecular arrays (best near-term balance)
2. Engineered phase-phonon crystals (high-control backup path)
3. Hybrid bioelectric+d-orbital interfaces (long-horizon high-risk path)
